About

A quick overview of my background and interests.

I’m a computational chemist and scientific modeller with a strong interest in using numerical methods, simulations, and modern software practices to understand complex chemical systems. My work spans molecular modelling, quantum chemistry, and data-driven analysis of simulation outputs.

I enjoy building reproducible, containerized workflows—using tools like Docker—to make scientific research easier to share, run, and scale. I’m particularly interested in how cloud-native technologies can support large-scale simulations and collaborative research.

Outside of work and study, I’m continuously learning through online courses and hands-on projects, refining both my scientific and software engineering skills.

Current focus: strengthening my expertise in containerization (Docker), orchestration (Kubernetes), and modern DevOps practices for scientific computing—while deepening my understanding of advanced molecular modelling techniques.
